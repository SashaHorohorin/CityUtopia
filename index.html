Milet
hippodamian system | 485 BC
> political
crisis: political

geometrical layout based on health, economy and defenebility

description:
  • - the uniformity of the urban layout represents the equality of citizens in greek democracy
  • - provided for a twofold threefold division of the city into sacred, public and private areas for commercially, agriculturally and militarily active citizens.
  • - main and side streets are crossed at right angles and delimit the residential blocks or insulae
  • - each insula is divided into plots of equal size
  • - the uniform street pattern is determined by the closed walls of the residential buildings
  • - for sacred and public squares and buildings, areas are left out of the basic grid that correspond to one, two or more insulae.

> economical
